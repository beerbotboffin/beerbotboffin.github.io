Wifi Controlled Magnetic Stirrer

Wifi Controlled Magnetic Stirrer

With heater and Wifi!, 8 Jul, 2020

I sometimes make my printed circuit boards and need a way to Tin plate them.

I've a got a recipe, but need a way to heat & mix the solutions - what I need is known as a magnetic stirrer. They exist, and not overly expensive from the usual places in Mao's Dollarama (© AvE). But I wanted to make my own.

I started by buying a 40W Silicone heater pad, 100mm dia Aluminium (yes, Aluminium), stirrer bars, beaker and an OLED display from ebay. I have a bunch of NodeMCU's in stock, so they weren't an issue.
Next up was designing an enclosure to 3D print. I'll upload the files to shittyverse when it's done.
I've been prototyping the firmware and learning KiCAD to design a PCB to mount the electronics. When the PCB design is finished, I'll either mill it on the small CNC or chemically etch the board. How do I plate it? Good question...

Yes, I know could just order the PCBs from somewhere like JLCPCB, but given that I'm an amateur electronics designer I often get it wrong and need to iterate. With the CNC mill I can (will) prototype and iterate a board in hours, not weeks.

Now, you won't see it until I make some more progress and post photos, but one of the design parameters is to keep it compact. Thus I decided on no externals controls (no switches, pots etc) and control the heating and RPM with a web interface via Wifi.

Stay tuned for more updates
